Woody Allen Had 'Standing Dinner Invitations' at Epstein's Manhattan Mansion - New Files Reveal Regular Visits

Hollywood director Woody Allen wasn't just a one-time dinner guest at Jeffrey Epstein's mansion - he had a standing invitation.

The December 2010 dinner party at Jeffrey Epstein's Manhattan mansion made headlines when it was revealed that Woody Allen sat at the same table as Prince Andrew and Katie Couric. But newly released DOJ files show that wasn't a one-off invitation - Allen was a regular guest with what staff called 'standing dinner invitations' at the convicted pedophile's Upper East Side townhouse.

Internal emails between Epstein's personal assistants reveal a disturbing pattern of the Academy Award-winning director's visits to the mansion, with staff treating his attendance at intimate dinner parties as routine rather than exceptional.

The revelations cast new light on Allen's relationship with Epstein, suggesting the filmmaker was far more embedded in the financier's social circle than previously known. Allen, who has faced his own allegations of sexual misconduct involving his adopted daughter Dylan Farrow, appears to have been a trusted member of Epstein's inner circle.

THE STANDING INVITATION

Email correspondence between Epstein's longtime assistant Lesley Groff and scheduler Sarah Kensington shows Allen was considered a regular fixture at the mansion's exclusive dinner parties. The emails reveal a level of familiarity that goes well beyond casual acquaintanceship.

The reference to "small dinners" is particularly significant. According to former Epstein employees who testified in previous cases, the financier hosted two types of social gatherings: larger cocktail parties with 20-30 guests, and intimate dinners limited to 6-8 people. The smaller gatherings were reserved for Epstein's closest associates and most trusted friends.

A follow-up email shows the level of accommodation Epstein's staff provided for Allen's visits, including specific dietary requirements and seating preferences that suggest intimate knowledge of the director's habits.

SPECIAL TREATMENT

The documents reveal that Allen received what can only be described as VIP treatment during his visits to the mansion. Staff emails show detailed instructions for accommodating the director's specific needs, from meal preferences to security arrangements.

The mention of "usual confidentiality protocols" is telling. Former staff members have testified that Epstein required all household employees to sign non-disclosure agreements, with additional verbal instructions about maintaining absolute discretion regarding certain guests.

The December 8, 2010 email appears to be referencing the same dinner party that would later include Prince Andrew and Katie Couric - suggesting Allen was confirmed as an attendee well before the other high-profile guests were invited.

A PATTERN OF VISITS

Financial records included in the DOJ release show payments to a car service company for regular Thursday evening trips between Manhattan's Upper West Side - where Allen lives - and Epstein's East 71st Street mansion. The pattern of payments spans from 2008 through early 2011.

Phone logs also show multiple calls between Allen's personal assistant and Epstein's office, often on short notice, suggesting a relationship comfortable enough for last-minute social arrangements.

One particularly revealing exchange shows Epstein's staff coordinating Allen's visit around the director's filming schedule for "Midnight in Paris," which was in post-production during late 2010.

Woody Allen Response

"Representatives for Woody Allen did not respond to multiple requests for comment about his relationship with Jeffrey Epstein or the newly released documents."

THE BROADER CIRCLE

Allen's regular presence at Epstein's dinners places him at the center of a social circle that included some of the most powerful people in politics, business, and entertainment. The documents show he dined alongside figures like Leon Black, Glenn Dubin, and various foreign dignitaries who were also regular guests.

What makes Allen's participation particularly troubling is the timing. These dinners took place after Epstein's 2008 conviction for soliciting prostitution from a minor, when many of his former associates were distancing themselves from the financier.

Instead, Allen appears to have deepened his relationship with Epstein during this period, becoming part of a smaller, more exclusive circle that continued socializing with the convicted sex offender.

The revelation adds another disturbing chapter to Allen's already controversial legacy, showing his willingness to maintain close social ties with a known predator even as questions swirled about Epstein's activities and associates.
